**Utilization and Capacity.** The Proteomics Core operates at capacity. The facility maintains three mass spectrometers — a Bruker timsTOF HT, a Thermo Fusion Lumos, and a Thermo Exploris 480 — but only the timsTOF HT represents current-generation technology. Three analytical staff process approximately 400 service orders per year ($850K–$980K revenue) for 135–176 active PI groups, with a consistent 1–2 week turnaround and 62 orders currently in queue. External academic demand has doubled from 16% to 35% of revenue in two years, and the facility serves 320 unique PI groups across 104+ institutions. The core is not turning away work, but all demand for state-of-the-art proteomics workflows funnels through the single timsTOF HT, which represents both the primary bottleneck and a single point of failure. Staffing leaves no buffer for absences or method development. A new high-performance instrument would directly expand throughput, retire dependence on aging platforms, and enable capabilities the growing user base demands.
