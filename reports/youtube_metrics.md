## Impact Narrative (for grant applications)

Brett Phinney's YouTube channel is one of the most-watched proteomics educational resources in the field, with **37,149 views and 3,424 hours of watch time** across 37 tutorial videos in under 3 years. The top video — a DIA-NN data analysis tutorial — alone has attracted **12,113 views and 887 hours of engaged watch time**, making it one of the most-viewed DIA proteomics tutorials on YouTube.

The channel directly supports the Core's mission of lowering barriers to proteomics adoption: researchers who watch these tutorials are better prepared to design experiments, interpret results, and leverage the Core's capabilities. The 618 subscribers represent an engaged global audience of proteomics practitioners who rely on the Core director's expertise for software training that is not available from instrument vendors.

Key educational contributions include the first publicly available tutorials on:
- DIA-NN analysis workflow (12k+ views)
- Skyline integration for DIA data visualization (4k views)
- FragPipe vs Spectronaut vs DIA-NN head-to-head comparison (2.4k views)
- Running DIA-NN on HPC clusters via SLURM (600 views)
- Differential expression with Limpa (1.4k views in 6 months)
- DE-LIMP open-source proteomics pipeline (new, 300 views in 2 weeks)
