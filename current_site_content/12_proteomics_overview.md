# Proteomics Overview — proteomics.ucdavis.edu/proteomics-overview

## What is Bottom-Up (Shotgun) Proteomics?
Large-scale study of protein structure and function using mass spectrometry.

## Proteomics vs. RNA-Seq
- **RNA-Seq:** Measures genetic instructions (mRNA), easier but only ~50% correlation to actual protein levels
- **Proteomics:** Measures functional proteins directly, detecting modifications and interactions

## The Six-Step Workflow

1. **Protein Extraction** — Cells are lysed, proteins denatured with urea/SDS, and disulfide bonds broken
2. **Digestion** — Trypsin enzyme cuts proteins into peptides after arginine/lysine residues
3. **Liquid Chromatography** — HPLC separates peptides by hydrophobicity over 60–120 minutes
4. **Ionization** — Electrospray ionization creates gas-phase charged peptides
5. **Mass Spectrometry** — Two scan types: MS1 (survey) and MS2 (tandem) measure mass-to-charge ratios and identify sequences
6. **Data Analysis** — Search engines match experimental spectra against protein databases

## Emerging Technologies
- **DIA (Data-Independent Acquisition):** Analyzes all peptides simultaneously rather than selecting only abundant ones, enabling comprehensive proteome coverage with AI assistance
- **Single-Cell Proteomics:** Measures ~6,500 proteins per individual cell, surpassing RNA-seq's molecular insights
