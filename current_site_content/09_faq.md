# FAQ — proteomics.ucdavis.edu/faq

## How do I Start a Sample Submission?
Contact the Core via email. You'll need an active PPMS account, properly labeled samples, and must complete a submission form. See the Submissions page for details.

## What is the typical turnaround time for sample analysis?
Turnaround time for peptide samples is usually 2 weeks for on-campus users. Turnaround time for protein samples is usually 3 weeks, depending on experiment type and LCMS availability.

## What is the typical per-sample cost? How do we get an estimate?
Visit the Prices page for standard per-sample costs. Expenses generally decrease when submitting larger sample batches, with discounts discussed before submission.

## How is LCMS billed?
LCMS runs are priced by the hour, not by how many samples are run.

## How much does Enrichment Cost?
Enrichment is $50 per sample additional cost.

## Why is my protein not found when it is seen in Western Blot?
Possible reasons: the Western Blot technique may be more sensitive than mass spectrometry, or the Western Blot antibody may not be binding the intended protein.

## What can I do to reduce processing time?
Complete all submission steps beforehand. Fill in all available fields on the sample sheet, particularly buffer composition, organism, and UniProt ID.

## What is the best way to ship my samples?
Use FedEx (US) or DHL instead of USPS. Notify the facility of shipment. Avoid Friday shipping. Dry peptides tolerate ice packs or room temperature; never ship beads frozen or on dry ice as this causes contamination.

## I am having trouble submitting a Sample Submission Sheet?
Either the submitter or the PI needs to have an active PPMS account associated with the email entered in the sheet.
